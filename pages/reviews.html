Crimson Desert
Open WorldAction RPGPearl Abyss2025

Pearl Abyss promised a brutal, beautiful open world, and Crimson Desert delivers on almost every front. This is not just another action RPG — it is a statement from a studio that knows exactly what it is building.

The Highs
Combat That Demands Respect
Every fight in Crimson Desert feels earned. The weight behind each strike and the fluidity of the dodge system means you cannot just mash your way through.

A World Worth Getting Lost In
Pywel is enormous and gorgeous. Every corner of the map feels handcrafted and exploration is rewarded constantly.
The Lows
Performance at Launch
Even on high-end hardware, Crimson Desert can stutter during large-scale encounters. Patching has been consistent but optimisation is still ongoing.

Pacing Dips in the Mid-Game
The opening hours are relentless but the middle section of the story loses some momentum.
Final Thoughts

Crimson Desert is the game Pearl Abyss has been building toward for years. The combat is exceptional, the world is stunning, and Kliff is a protagonist worth following. One of the best action RPGs to release in years. If you have been on the fence — get in the Paradox.

Warframe
Action RPGFree to PlayCo-op11+ Years

Having been a Tenno for over eleven years, I've seen Warframe evolve from a small co-op experiment into one of the most unique online games out there. After more than a decade, it still has a magic that few games manage to keep alive.

The Highs
Fluid Movement That Still Feels Fresh
The parkour system is still the heart of Warframe. Even after thousands of hours it never feels old.

Cinematic Quests Worth Playing
The Second Dream, The War Within, and The New War are still some of the most memorable experiences I have had in gaming.
The Lows
The Grind Can Be Overwhelming
Farming for rare parts or mods has always been part of Warframe, but it can wear players down.

Accessibility for New Players
After eleven years of updates, the learning curve is higher than ever.
Final Thoughts

After eleven years I am still proud to call myself a Tenno, and I do not see that changing anytime soon. One of the most ambitious and rewarding games out there.

8.5
Borderlands 4
Looter ShooterGearboxCo-op

Borderlands 4 feels both familiar and refreshingly new. Gearbox is aiming to evolve the formula, and as someone who has spent hundreds of hours farming legendaries, this entry delivers.

The Highs
Movement Evolves the Mayhem
Double jumping, grappling, dodging, and gliding adds a new layer of energy. Fights feel faster and more vertical than ever.

Loot Still Rules
Wild weapon designs and the thrill of the chase keep you hooked.
The Lows
Story Still Secondary
After the highs of Borderlands 2, Gearbox still struggles to recapture that magic.

Overwhelming for New Players
The game throws a lot at you quickly.
Final Thoughts

Borderlands 4 delivers what veterans wanted — louder, faster, and more chaotic. The new movement and build variety breathe life into the series. For a Vault Hunter, that is more than enough.

5.5
Destiny 2: Edge of Fate
Looter ShooterBungieLive Service

Having been a Guardian since 2014, I've lived through every chapter of Bungie's saga. With Edge of Fate, it feels both like a reset and a reminder of what this franchise can still deliver.

The Highs
A Story Worth Caring About
Edge of Fate finally gives us a narrative hook worthy of the universe.

New Powers
The new ability systems add genuine freshness to a game that has needed it.
The Lows
The Old Problems Aren't Gone
Too many zones tread familiar ground despite the new coat of paint.

Mechanics That Divide
Some new systems feel forced rather than natural additions.
Final Thoughts

A solid step, not a triumphant leap. If you've stood by Destiny through every expansion, you'll find plenty here to appreciate and plenty to critique.

6.5
Path of Exile
ARPGFree to PlayGrinding Gear

Path of Exile felt overwhelming at first. The world is dark, brutal, and complex, and the game doesn't hold your hand. But the more I played, the more I began to see why so many people have been hooked for years.

The Highs
Endless Build Possibilities
No two characters are ever the same. Each level felt like progress toward something uniquely mine.

Loot and Crafting That Feels Meaningful
Every drop matters. The crafting system rewards knowledge and patience.
The Lows
An Intimidating Learning Curve
There's no gentle introduction. Systems pile on quickly.

Performance Hiccups
Even on decent hardware, performance dips happen during heavy fights.
Final Thoughts

Path of Exile was a challenge, but one that grew on me with every hour. Rough around the edges, but beneath that is a masterpiece waiting to be discovered.
